# 8-Week Strength Foundation Plan

## Overview

This plan is designed for intermediate trainees building a consistent strength base. Each phase
has specific joint-angle targets measured by the CoachCam pose tracker. When you ask for
feedback, mention your current week so the AI can reference the right targets.

---

## Week 1-2: Movement Quality (Light Load)

Focus: Establish clean patterns before adding load. Technique over everything.

- **Squat**: Target depth 90-100° knee angle. Priority: back neutral, knees track over toes.
  Load should feel easy — you are here to groove the movement.
- **Bicep curl**: Full range 30-160°. No momentum — controlled 3s down.
  Keep elbow pinned at your side throughout.
- **Shoulder press**: Elbows at 90° at start, push to ~170° lockout. Don't arch back.
  If you cannot reach 170° without lumbar extension, reduce load.
- **Push-up**: Elbows at ~80° at the bottom. Plank body position throughout.
- **Deadlift**: Hip hinge pattern — aim for 90-110° hip angle at bottom.
  Bar stays over mid-foot. Drive hips to lockout at 175°+.

---

## Week 3-4: Building Volume

Focus: Add a third set to each primary lift. Maintain the Week 1-2 angle standards.

- **Squat**: Maintain 85-100° knee angle. Add 3rd set. Note: if knee angle exceeds 110°,
  you are not going deep enough — prioritize depth before load.
- **Bicep curl**: Same 30-160° range. Increase load only if form holds over all 3 sets.
- **Shoulder press**: Maintain 90° start, 170° lockout. Increase load by 2.5kg maximum.
- **Push-up**: Elbows should reach 75-80° at bottom. If you cannot hit 75°, regress to
  an incline variation.
- **Deadlift**: Hip hinge — aim for 140-160° hip angle at lockout. Shins vertical at setup.

---

## Week 5-6: Strength Focus

Focus: Heavier loads, same movement standards. Patience under load is the skill.

- **Squat**: 80-90° is now the target — full depth required. If you stall above 90°,
  reduce load by 10% and rebuild depth.
- **Bicep curl**: 35-155° acceptable range at heavier loads. No cheating reps.
- **Shoulder press**: 85° start, 165° lockout minimum. Note: at higher loads, small
  reduction in lockout range (165° vs 170°) is acceptable. Any lower and reduce weight.
- **Push-up**: Elbows should reach ~75° at bottom. Slower pace — 3s down, 1s pause, push.
- **Deadlift**: 145-160° hip angle at lockout. Reset between reps if needed.

---

## Week 7-8: Peak

Focus: Demonstrate everything you have built. Prioritize form over additional load.

- **Squat**: 80-95° with controlled descent (3 seconds down). This is the benchmark.
  If you miss depth, this is feedback from your body — not a failure.
- **Bicep curl**: Full 30-160° range. Reduce load rather than shortening range.
- **Shoulder press**: 170° lockout target returns. Any load that prevents this is too heavy.
- **Push-up**: 70-80° elbow angle at bottom, 3s down, pause, explode up.
- **Deadlift**: Clean lockout at 175°+. No hitching. Hip hinge stays the priority.

## General Principles

- Rest 2-3 minutes between heavy sets.
- If any joint angle is outside the target range for 3+ consecutive sessions, regress load.
- Controlled descent is non-negotiable on all lifts — rushing the eccentric is where injuries happen.
- The angle targets are guides, not hard limits. Mobility varies by individual. Work toward them.
